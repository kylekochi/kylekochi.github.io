Overview

Co-designed a 3D-printable prosthetic socket system with adjustable volume and scalable fit for transradial amputees. The device enables consistent EMG signal acquisition for myoelectric control across daily fluctuations in residual limb volume — a persistent challenge in prosthetic fitting that limits device adoption and comfort.

What I Built
  • Parametric CAD model in OnShape supporting custom limb geometries and rapid patient-specific fabrication.
  • Novel lacing mechanism and sleeve system engineered to accommodate daily limb volume changes of ±3% while maintaining consistent electrode contact for EMG acquisition.
  • FEA simulations to identify high-stress regions in the socket geometry, iterating the design to improve structural performance under real-world loading.
  • Full fabrication pipeline using FDM 3D printing, enabling custom socket production with a turnaround under 24 hours per unit.
