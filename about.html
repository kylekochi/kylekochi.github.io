About

Engineer.

Builder.

I'm Kyle — a mechatronics engineer from Vancouver, recently graduated from UBC. I build systems where mechanical design, electronics, and software intersect: robots that run labs overnight, prosthetic arms that respond to muscle signals, and hardware that works reliably when it actually matters.

The problems I enjoy most often fall under multiple disciplines. A robotic mixing station can be a mechanical problem until sensors fail. A prosthetic hand can be an electrical problem until the firmware breaks. Sometimes, its not even a technical problem. Shipping something that works in the real world means caring about the person on the other end, and I love to keep the human element alive in my work.

My time spent playing baseball at UBC while studying engineering and building robots on the side reflects how I approach real-world problems — the opportunity to compete seriously at something physical while completing my degree and running a robotics team shaped how I think about technical work. A good team that shows up consistently and work through setbacks together, will have a much greater ability to execute and deliver under pressure. That mentality is something I've learned on the field, but I believe the same priniciples apply to much more than just sports.

The Thread

Most of my projects share something: they were built to be used.

  • The robotic mixing station I built at the Berlinguette Research Group ran over 100 autonomous cycles and was used daily by more than 30 researchers.
  • The prosthetic arm competed at CYBATHLON.
  • The adjustable socket was tested on a real limb model.

I'm drawn to problems where the gap between "working in the lab" and "working in the world" is hard to close — problems that require caring about tolerances, reliability, and the person on the other end.

Long term, I'm interested in building robotic systems that move beyond controlled environments and into the real world.
