Overview

The ADA-CO2 project is a self-driving laboratory built for autonomous discovery of CO₂ electrolysis materials at the Berlinguette Research Group, UBC. I joined as a Mechatronics Engineer Co-op, responsible for designing and deploying the electromechanical subsystems that expanded the platform from a single-station setup to an 8× larger multi-station robotic workspace.

What I Built
  • Robotic vial mixing module — mechanical fixtures, pneumatic actuator, integrated with UR5e robot. Eliminated 1 hour of manual lab prep per experiment cycle.
  • Custom digital I/O control box — KiCAD schematic, CNC-machined aluminum enclosure, 12V relay interface for plug-and-play integration of off-the-shelf lab equipment. Reduced device integration time by 70%.
  • Pneumatic distribution panels supporting electrolyzer rigs, spray coaters, and mixing modules — designed for modular expansion without downtime.
  • Linear cobot track integration — expanded the robot's reachable workspace over 8×, enabling future growth in research throughput.
  • Python automation scripts for robot sequencing, data logging, and vial decapping routines.
